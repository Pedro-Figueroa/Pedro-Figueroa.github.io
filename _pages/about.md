I'm a second-year PhD student at the Department of Earth & Environment in Boston Univeristy, working with [Prof. Xiaozhou Ruan](https://xiaozhour.github.io). My research focuses on the physical oceanography of coastal areas, specially on flow-topography interactions. Currently I am studiying the effects of submarine canyons on the circulation and cross-shore transports along Eastern Boundary Upwelling systems, using process-oriented numerical simulations.

I earned my B.S and professional certification from the Universidad de Concepción, working on submesoscale instabilities at upwelling fronts with Prof. Oscar Pizarro in the Department of Geophysics. After that, I earned my M.S in Oceanography at the Department of Oceanography, working on Interannual variability in fjord of the Chilean Patagonia with Prof. Fabían Tapia. Following this, I worked as a research assistant at the Universidad del Bío-Bío with [Prof. Gonzalo Saldías](https://gsaldias.github.io), working primarily in submarine canyons.

I'm always interested in working on new projects, learn new things, and talk about science. Feel free to contact me if you want to work together, have questions about my research or just want to discuss potential ideas.
